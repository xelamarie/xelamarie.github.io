Audience

Whether it's local folks wanting to plan out their day/week, or visitors to the area needing information and guidance, Winds in the East will be thier number one source for local weather. More broadly, our target audience is all adults with access to the internet who live in, live near, visit, or who have interest in the Preston/Soda Springs/Fish Haven, Idaho area. It will target not only those wanting to know current weather conditions, but also those who would like recommendations of what to do in the area. It will be for people seeking to feel a sense of community as well.

Scenarios

  • What kind of shoes should I wear today?
  • When should I blow out my sprinklers so the pipes don't freeze?
  • Are other people experiencing a power outage right now?
  • What is the best time of year to visit the local Hot Springs?
  • Are there any outdoor events around Christmastime?
  • What kids of plants grow best in this climate?
  • Are the hiking trails still covered in snow?

Personas

1. Wendy Fullerton is 60-something retired choir teacher who is living in Preston, Idaho with her husband of 45 years. She spends several hours a day on her iPad, giving lots of time and attention to a cappella videos on YouTube and to her coloring app. She would like to get out of the house more, both for social fulfillment and for the exercise. She has also recently noticed that her two dogs are getting chubby and need to be taken on more walks. Wendy has begun to check the weather daily in order to prepare for her outdoor excursions and has been using the internet to get recommendations for community activities.

2. Clark Magpie just turned 40 years old and is living in Soda Springs, Idaho with his family. He has recently reloacted to this small Idaho town after spending most of his life in metropolitan areas. When he is not working or serving in his church, he is helping his wife raise and entertain 4 girls and 2 boys (twins). Clark is a country boy at heart and is thrilled to now have the outdoors at his front door! He is looking forward to taking his kids fishing, snowmobiling, hiking, and spelunking in Caribou County. Clark relies heavily on his smartphone to keep track of all his work and church responsibilities, so that is usually how he accesses the internet for other business and leisure as well. He plans to use his phone multiple times a week to check the weather and coordinate his outdoor adventures accordingly.
